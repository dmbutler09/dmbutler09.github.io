It's a SNAKE! Wait... no it's not.

STEP 1

I went for a simpler approach on this project and wanted to create something like what is pictured above. As usual I started out with a sketch. I planned to use ball joints at first, but after a meeting with Joshua, I decided to use the rivets provided in class. I thought it would be easier. While doing this sketching and planning phase, I had to measure a lot more pieces than I originally thought.

STEP 2

After sketching, it was time to build the snake in OnShape. OnShape is a WAAAYYY easier tool than Rhino. However, I still ran into trouble when it came to simple tasks like panning around the screen and altering parts that I had already made. Some parts of the shape aren't exactly how I want them to look, but the overall shape is right and the rivets should fit in their appropriate spaces.

You can download the source file here.
STL 3D print ready file

STEP 3

I set up my .stl file in Dremel and found out it'd take a whopping 13 hours to print 4 of my pieces. Unfortunately, I did not have time to print the project before class. I even tried adjusting the density and support settings, but could only change the print time by an hour at most.

STEP 4

I played around with the assembly in OnShape, but as intuitive as the program is, it couldn't conceive of how I'd put these pieces together... or I couldn't figure out the correct way to tell it to put them together. My thought is that the outcropping pieces will stack on the ends of each piece. The outcropping pieces would alternate at the joints, piece A, piece B, piece A2, piece B2.
