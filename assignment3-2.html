Lotus Flower Lamp!

STEP 1

Another ambitious design! This time I wanted to make a lamp that resembled a lotus flower. My first step was sketching out how this lamp would look and how the pieces would fit together. I wanted 8 leaves that would plug into a base piece that wrapped around the lamp. This design developed as I completed the other steps.

STEP 2

Now to realize these designs in rhino. What I envisioned was a wooden base with 3d printed parts that would attach. The hardest part of this was learning to create 3D objects in rhino, especially the leaves. I started with flat leaves and built them up to 3D images.

STEP 3

The next step was creating the base that these leaves would plug. This was easier that the leaves because Rhino already has commands to create square shapes.

STEP 4

Time for printing! I started with the base. It took a lot of test prints to get the pieces to fit together and to make sure the petals would fit into their appropriate slots. Until finally, I had the base at a size that worked. I switched from cardboard to wood during this testing process.

Laser Settings for cardboard: Speed 25%, Power 100%, Frequency: 100%

Laser Settings for wood: Speed 6%, Power 100%, Frequency 100% (I forgot to take the pictures >_<)

STEP 5

Now to print the petals. I exported the one of each type of petal to Dremel and adjusted them to print multiple pieces in one session. In Dremel, my imported petals were way too small. I believe the program converted the inches OI had in Rhino to milimeters. So I had to scale the pieces by 2540%. This was frustrating and satisyfing. My small petal print worked perfectly, my large petals were ruined the first time around.

Fun with printing

STEP 6

CONSTRUCTION!! The connections between the base and the petals are really tight, but the connections between the bases are not. Some other changes I would make would be making the petals thinner so they will print faster and change the design of the base so it is a solid piece. THANK YOUS!
