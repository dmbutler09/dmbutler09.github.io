Dice Box Final

STEP 1

For my last trick! I want to make a dice box for my D&D games. I want it to have a lid and a partition to hold dice. I found a picture online that is close to what I want and started there. Then I created a sketch of what I wnt taking into account the measurements of what I need. The box will be 8x8in with a 2in partition for dice.

STEP 2

The next part was re-creating the sketch in OnShape and making sure the pieces fit together online. I had to make a few changes to how I envisioned the lid woirking. In my sketch I imagined a 3D printed rod to act as the moving part but in OnShape I designed for the rivets that we received earlier this quarter. After designing in OnShape I exported the individual pieces to DXF files and combined them into a PDF for easy laser cutting. The next step is laser cutting which I will be doing tonight.

STEP 3

Step 3 was my tolerance test before I actually printed out all of my pieces, and I'm glad I did it because the tolerances were WAY off. Initially the female parts were .51 x .135 in. while the male were .125 x .5 in. This made theoretical sense, but not practical. I ended up having to go back to OnShape and changing the female parts to .465 x .116 in. Also I learned in this step that my usual laser cut settings didn't work. When I ran the first tolerance cut the laser did not cut all the way through the wood. The new settings I would need were:

Speed: 10% Power: 100% Frequency: 100%

STEP 4

Now to cut the actual pieces. One of the laser cutters in the Mill was not working so I had to cut my pieces on two different pieces of wood instead one big piece like I planned. No biggie, the Mill staff were able to exchange the wood I bought for 2 of the smaller size with no problem.

STEP 4

STEP 5

My tolerance test betrayed me. Even though the test plug I printed fit into the smallest sized hole, the actual pieces did not fit at all. I thought I'd have to reprint the bottom and top, BUT i decided to give sanding a try first. AND IT WORKED!!! I folded some sand paper really small and was able to make the holes in the bottom and top just a little bigger.

STEP 6

Now that everything fits it was time to assemble this sucker. Which was pretty easy. Unfortunately, I am only human so my sanding job was imperfect so some of the pieces fit very tightly, others not so tightly, but still a good fit all around.
