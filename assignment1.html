DB! (Press Fit Construction Kit)

STEP 1

I began this project by deciding on the shape I wanted for my kit. I decided to usual my initals because I couldn't think of a creative shape and these were readily available. I didn't really consider the size I wanted the shapes to be. I focused on how I wanted them to fit together. I used divets that sick out and cut into the 'D' and 'B' shapes.

STEP 2

The next steps were to test the prints. The first set was about 4x4 in., but didn't hold together very well. I also didn't want each piece to be huge. I wanted something I could easily hold together. I shrunk the shapes to about 3x3 in. Then I changed the the parts that stick out to be slightly larger that the parts that go in. (.52 in. & .47 in. respectively)

STEP 3

After a few fitting tests it was time for production and to build the weird stuff you see below. My laser settings on the left show the speed at 25%, while power and frequency were both at 100%. Except for a few rare areas the laser always cut all the way through.
