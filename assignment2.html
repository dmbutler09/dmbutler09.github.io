CAD Model & Production

STEP 1

This assignment started rather ambitiously. I didn't want to use the same objects I used in assignment 1. The sketch here shows 4 distinct pieces with most of the object being made of the polygons at the bottom.

STEP 2

Once the sketching was done, I needed to create the shapes in rhino. I quickly saw all of the differences between this and other visual design software I had used. I spent most of my time looking for tools to do simple tasks like curve corners and mirror shapes. So I simplified my idea into just the polygon. Before I could move on to cutting I had to do a bit of resizing of my incisions because I forgot to adjust my units in Rhino. I did this resizing in Illustrator. When I tried to do it in Rhino I broke my original design and had to recreate it.

Special thanks to Lukas, Julie D., and Joshua for all of their help Rhino and Grasshopper.

STEP 3

Just like in the first assignment I did a few test cuts to see how my pieces would fit together and how big they would actually be. I also took advantage of being able to test where the laser cutter would cut my material by running my job with the lid open. This helped me save material and run the cutter fewer times than in the first assignment. I used the same laser settings, but noticed a few more flames this time than last. In future assignments I may change my laser cutting settings.

STEP 4

The final product worked very well and passes the shake test. The notches are a tight fit around the block.
